### Turn and face the strange

Mostly arose from a fun thought I had recently while watching ALttP: how many screens have changed in the NMG speedrun since I (JoshRTA) started playing the game back in late August of 2013.

### Some guidelines

1. This is all arbitrary. Deal with it!
1. I generally ignore what I'll call "idiostrats" (for 'idiom' not 'idiot' lol)--or strats that an individual player (or very small number of players) does that no one else does. Even if/when you could potentially argue more people should do it (acmlm's Hera Tile Room and fsg's various framerule gambles are good examples).
1. I don't count things people aren't doing in runs at all (Swamp bomb spooky is a good example).
1. I *do* count what I'll call "meta" changes. These are changes in technique and approach that wouldn't scan as new or unfamiliar to players in 2013, but *would* scan as an incorrect or unusual way to play. An example of this includes the trend towards moving Link diagonally over a 1 tile distance when walking to a perpendicular dash route.
1. I generally don't count a change in knowledge of a screen unless it materially/practically affects the way runners play the room. An example of this type of change off the top of my head is North Turtles in POD. We empirically know it doesn't work all of the time, but no one was doing it anyway.

### Segment changes

Some segments have a change with a larger blast radius than a single screen. Examples of this include the new POD hover route or Blank Smiths.

In these cases, I just say "xyz" change occurred and applies to this segment as a whole. It doesn't really make sense to think of them as things that have impacted single rooms/screens.

### Global Changes

I'm also going to apply three global changes to the run as a whole:
1. Optimized crystal catches: I could say each of the boss rooms in the Dark World has changed because of this, but that feels silly to me. I'm just going to count this as one change over the whole run.
1. The meta shift on where to menu. Back in the Krystal era, players would frequently use menus to aid with dashes having a bonk risk. This usage has mostly fallen out of favor in the modern game. Counting this in terms of individual screens is pretty difficult, and menus are a big source of runner idioms. But this shift is definitely present. Watch one of Krystal's runs, and you'll be losing your mind at the places she chooses to menu.
1. The meta shift on general movement and nudging when entering a Link-width gap (such as a door for example). In the modern game, players bias themselves to being early because of how slow overshooting is. This was not in anyone's movement calculus back in the day.
